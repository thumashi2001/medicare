1. The "Join" Button Logic
In the Appointment List, both users(Doctor/Patient) see their scheduled slots. For each appointment it has a button for video conference such as “Join video call”
•	The system doesn't need to generate a new meeting link. It simply uses the Appointment ID (e.g., APP-9987) as the unique "Room Name" for Agora.
2. The Step-by-Step User Flow
Step A: The Click
When the Patient (or Doctor) clicks the button, the app doesn't just open the camera. It redirects them to a new URL in your React app, for example: myhealthapp.com/video-room/appointment-12345
Step B: The Waiting Room (The Separate Page)
Once they arrive at this new page, they are in the Waiting Room. This is a crucial "buffer" zone where:
•	Hardware Check: The user sees a small preview of their own camera. They can check if their hair looks okay and if their microphone is working.
•	Permission: The browser asks, "Allow this website to use your camera and mic?"
•	The "Ready" Button: There is a big button that says "Enter Consultation."
Step C: The Live Consultation
Once they click "Enter," the Agora video stream starts.
•	Patient Side: They see the Doctor’s face large and their own face in a small corner.
•	Doctor Side: They see the Patient’s face large and their own face in a small corner.
Step D: Ending part
•	Both side have a leave button
